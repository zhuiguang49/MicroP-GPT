Thine eyes I love, and they, as pitying me,
Knowing thy heart torment me with disdain,
Have put on black, and loving mourners be,
As I have betaken thee every sweetness
To mingle him with tresses growing round me.
No kiss, no love missed shall mine sweet soul walk
Upon these golden nights,
To dig nor please, so keen that to sigh he dies
Thy own hate may buy me no bliss,
Too much fixed for strife's sake.
  My thoughts are love and love are love's shadows;
  I am not cast aside, but fell asleep, forsaken,
  By my ungodliness deceiving them,
  Thus if I were my self, what light would have faded?

Beshrew that heart that makes my heart to groan
For that deep wound it gives my friend and me.
Is 't not enough to torture me alone,
So first teach my dearest a lesson?
Cavate for me his flattery,
