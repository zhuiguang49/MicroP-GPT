When my love swears that she is made of truth,
I do believe her, though I know she lies,
That she might think me some untutored youth,
And that then I might love a fairer man.
If I were so aught, none would so learn
As a true reader. He that loves truth will say,
"These things thou knowing'st, are written in
Thy breast and in mine own eyes."
But he that dost confute
What thou supposest to be so,
Is not yet worthy to have his opinion
Of love.  So even thou shalt not lie,
But even my love will one day tell
Thou shouldst not treat as an untaught child.

Two loves I have, of comfort and despair,
That like two spirits do suggest me still.
My better angel is a man right fair,
And by his counsels all these poor afflicted are
Shewing how much they can with patience,
And in patience gentle applications
Have lain heavy on my side since my days.
A lover that I have, like two spirits in  
A county long dark, living as till some sunder
Is shut out from light and day by his kind
Who but daily looks out to wish for his gentle woe.
  And I, like his twin in bliss, who live,
  Too proud to fear my weakness.
